Abstract:
This work explores the intersection of neuroscience, deep learning, and music in-
formation retrieval by developing a system that generates music from electroen-
cephalogram signals. The system captures emotional states through EEG record-
ings during controlled emotion-induction sessions, processes these signals to ex-
tract valence and arousal features, and feeds them into a deep generative model
to produce emotionally coherent music. Ethical data collection standards ensured
participant wellbeing through time-constrained stimuli exposure and informed con-
sent, while UK GDPR compliance was maintained through anonymised storage.
The system was evaluated through both objective metrics, including training loss
and end-to-end latency, and subjective appraisal through Likert-scale listening
tests measuring the generated music’s capacity to express emotion. A Turing-
style test further assessed how generated outputs are perceived by participants.
Environmental impact was minimised through low-power equipment and consid-
erate use of cloud computing resources. This work contributes to the emerging
field of Brain-Computer Interfaces for creative and therapeutic applications, with
implications for personalised music therapy and affective computing.

AutoHome allows you to set your privacy preferences to your liking.

ADDING DEVICES
here you can add devices using your smart plugs, manually or using the QR codes provided. Again, if you don’t own any smart plugs this can still be done to schedule your device usage and tasks to create an energy-efficient routine.
